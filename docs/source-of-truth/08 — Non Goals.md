# Introduction

Every successful product says "no" more often than it says "yes".

HourWise cannot solve every transport problem.

Attempting to do so would create unnecessary complexity, increase maintenance costs and reduce the quality of the core platform.

This document defines the boundaries of HourWise.

Ideas outside these boundaries are not necessarily bad ideas.

They may become:

- future integrations
- partner products
- standalone businesses

But they should not automatically become part of HourWise.

---

# Core Principle

HourWise exists to improve:

- transport compliance
- operational awareness
- fleet intelligence
- administrative efficiency

Everything else should be questioned.

---

# HourWise Will Not Become...

---

## A Payroll System

HourWise supports payroll.

It records useful operational information.

It produces reports.

It highlights discrepancies.

Payroll calculations, taxation and employee payment remain the responsibility of specialist payroll software.

Integration is preferred over replacement.

---

## An Accounting Package

HourWise may generate invoices.

It may export expenses.

It may integrate with accounting software.

It should never replace bookkeeping, VAT returns, year-end accounts or financial management systems.

---

## A Human Resources Platform

HourWise may store:

- licences
- CPC
- driver cards
- medicals
- training records

It should not become an HR management system.

Recruitment, appraisals, leave management and personnel administration remain outside the platform.

---

## A General Fleet Tracking System

Location data should exist only where it provides operational value.

HourWise should not attempt to compete directly with dedicated telematics providers.

Instead:

Integrate.

Import.

Display.

Explain.

---

## A SatNav

Navigation is outside the scope of HourWise.

The platform may link to navigation software.

It should not replace specialist navigation products.

---

## A Generic Chatbot

Atlas is intentionally different.

Atlas exists to analyse fleet data and provide transport-specific intelligence.

It should not attempt to answer unrelated questions or become a general conversational assistant.

---

## A Document Editor

HourWise stores documents.

Indexes them.

Monitors expiry.

Links evidence.

It should not become Microsoft Word.

---

## A Spreadsheet Replacement

Reports should be exportable.

Users may still use spreadsheets.

HourWise should focus on producing better information rather than replacing spreadsheet software.

---

## A Social Network

Messaging supports operational communication.

HourWise should not become another WhatsApp, Facebook or Teams.

Social interaction is not a product goal.

---

## An Email Client

Email notifications remain useful.

HourWise should never attempt to replace email systems.

---

## A General Project Management Tool

Task management should exist only where directly related to transport compliance or fleet operations.

---

## An ERP Platform

Enterprise Resource Planning systems manage every aspect of a business.

HourWise should remain focused on transport operations and compliance.

---

# Features That Require Extra Scrutiny

Some ideas naturally sound attractive but require careful evaluation before inclusion.

Examples include:

Route planning

Scheduling

Recruitment

Training

Marketplace

Insurance

Vehicle purchasing

Fuel purchasing

Financial forecasting

These should only be added if they clearly strengthen the core platform.

---

# Separate Product Test

When considering a new feature, ask:

"If this feature became its own business, would it still make sense?"

If the answer is yes, it may deserve to become a separate product rather than part of HourWise.

---

# Integration First

Whenever practical:

Integrate with specialist software rather than replacing it.

HourWise should become the platform that connects transport operations.

Not the platform that attempts to own every workflow.

---

# Scope Control Questions

Before approving a feature ask:

Does it improve transport compliance?

Does it improve fleet operations?

Does it reduce administration?

Does it strengthen the ecosystem?

Would customers reasonably expect this inside HourWise?

Can another specialist product already do this better?

Would integration provide more value than replacement?

---

# Future Doesn't Mean Never

Items listed in this document are not permanently rejected.

They simply fall outside the current product vision.

Future versions of HourWise may revisit these decisions if:

Customer needs change.

Technology changes.

The ecosystem expands naturally.

The strategic direction evolves.

Until then, the default decision should remain "No".

---

# Product Boundary Statement

HourWise should remain focused on becoming the best transport compliance and fleet intelligence platform it can be.

Every feature should strengthen that mission.

Not distract from it.
