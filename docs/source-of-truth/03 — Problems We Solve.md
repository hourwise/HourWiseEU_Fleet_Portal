# Introduction

Software should never be built simply because an idea seems interesting.

Every feature should solve a genuine problem experienced by real users.

This document identifies those problems.

As HourWise evolves, every significant feature should reference one or more problems defined here.

If a proposed feature does not solve a meaningful problem, it should be challenged before development begins.

---

# Primary User Groups

HourWise primarily serves six groups.

- Professional Drivers
- Owner Drivers
- Transport Managers
- Fleet Operators
- Compliance Managers
- Business Owners

Each group experiences different challenges.

---

# Professional Drivers

## Problem 1 — Understanding Remaining Driving Time

Many drivers struggle to accurately calculate:

- Remaining driving time
- Remaining working time
- Required breaks
- Weekly driving limits
- Fortnightly driving limits

This uncertainty increases stress and creates unnecessary infringements.

### Desired Outcome

Drivers should understand their current legal position within seconds.

---

## Problem 2 — Paperwork

Drivers often complete:

- Vehicle checks
- Incident reports
- Expense claims
- Fuel records
- Mileage logs
- Driver statements

using paper forms or multiple disconnected apps.

### Desired Outcome

Daily administration should be completed digitally with minimal effort.

---

## Problem 3 — Poor Communication

Important information is often distributed through:

- WhatsApp
- Phone calls
- Text messages
- Printed notices

Messages become fragmented and difficult to track.

### Desired Outcome

Drivers receive important operational information in one place.

---

## Problem 4 — Compliance Anxiety

Drivers often worry about:

- remaining driving time
- infringements
- forgotten breaks
- document expiry

without knowing their actual compliance position.

### Desired Outcome

Drivers should feel informed rather than uncertain.

---

# Owner Drivers

## Problem 5 — Too Many Systems

Independent drivers frequently require separate software for:

- Hours
- Expenses
- Invoicing
- Reports
- Compliance

### Desired Outcome

A single application should manage daily business administration.

---

## Problem 6 — Administrative Burden

Owner drivers spend significant time:

- calculating invoices
- preparing records
- organising receipts
- tracking expenses

### Desired Outcome

Administrative work should be automated wherever practical.

---

# Transport Managers

## Problem 7 — Information Overload

Managers receive large amounts of operational data.

Finding the important information often requires considerable manual effort.

### Desired Outcome

The platform should surface only meaningful actions.

---

## Problem 8 — Reactive Compliance

Many compliance issues are discovered only after they have already occurred.

### Desired Outcome

Potential issues should be identified early enough for corrective action.

---

## Problem 9 — Preparing Audits

Audit preparation often involves gathering information from numerous systems.

### Desired Outcome

Evidence should already exist in a structured, searchable format.

---

# Fleet Operators

## Problem 10 — Fragmented Systems

Operators frequently purchase separate software for:

- Tachographs
- Maintenance
- Messaging
- Payroll
- Incidents
- Vehicle checks

### Desired Outcome

A connected platform reduces duplication and administration.

---

## Problem 11 — Limited Operational Visibility

Managers often lack a real-time understanding of:

- fleet health
- driver compliance
- vehicle status
- upcoming risks

### Desired Outcome

Operational priorities should be visible immediately after logging in.

---

# Compliance Managers

## Problem 12 — Document Management

Managing:

- licences
- CPC
- driver cards
- medicals
- inspections
- maintenance
- policies

can become extremely time consuming.

### Desired Outcome

The platform should monitor documentation automatically and provide timely reminders.

---

## Problem 13 — Evidence Collection

Demonstrating compliance frequently requires manual collection of records.

### Desired Outcome

Evidence should accumulate naturally as part of daily operations.

---

# Business Owners

## Problem 14 — Software Costs

Small operators often cannot justify expensive enterprise software.

### Desired Outcome

HourWise should remain accessible to businesses of all sizes.

---

## Problem 15 — Growth

Businesses often outgrow their software.

Changing systems is disruptive and expensive.

### Desired Outcome

HourWise should grow with the business rather than requiring replacement.

---

# Industry Problems

HourWise also addresses broader industry challenges.

## Increasing Regulation

Transport legislation continues to become more complex.

HourWise should simplify understanding rather than simply quoting legislation.

---

## Driver Shortages

Experienced transport professionals are increasingly difficult to recruit.

The software should help less experienced staff perform confidently.

---

## Digital Transformation

Many operators still depend heavily on spreadsheets and paper.

HourWise should modernise operations gradually without disrupting existing workflows.

# Product Design Rule

Every future feature must identify:

- Which problem it solves.
- Which user group benefits.
- Whether an existing feature already solves it.
- Why it belongs inside HourWise rather than a separate product.

Features that cannot clearly answer these questions should not progress to development.

---

# Success Statement

HourWise succeeds when users spend less time managing compliance and more time running their businesses safely, efficiently and confidently.
