The biggest opportunity

I think the biggest gap isn't another transport feature—it's turning Atlas into the automation layer that connects every part of the business.

Today, transport managers spend much of their day:

Reading emails.
Chasing paperwork.
Updating spreadsheets.
Checking expiry dates.
Creating reminders.
Copying information between systems.
Answering routine questions.
Looking for documents.
Following up outstanding actions.

Those are exactly the kinds of repetitive, rules-based tasks that an AI assistant can automate or dramatically reduce.

If you build Atlas as an operations platform rather than simply a chat interface, HourWise could evolve from being "fleet management software with AI" into an AI-first transport operating system. The transport modules become the trusted data source, while Atlas becomes the layer that interprets information, coordinates workflows, surfaces risks before they become problems, and automates much of the administrative work that currently consumes transport offices.

Looking back over everything we've designed together, I'd estimate HourWise already covers around 85–90% of what most premium fleet/compliance platforms offer. The remaining opportunity isn't to keep adding isolated modules; it's to connect them so that Atlas can reason across them. That's the part that could make HourWise feel fundamentally different from established competitors rather than simply matching their feature lists.I think it's definitely worth studying, but I wouldn't build Atlas around it.

From what I've learned about HourWise and where you're taking it, I think Strands solves a different problem. It's an open-source SDK from Strands Agents designed to make it easier to build AI agents and orchestrate tools, with support for multiple model providers, not just AWS.

My recommendation

I wouldn't adopt Strands today.

I would read through it carefully because it contains good architectural ideas and patterns.

I would design Atlas so that if, in two or three years' time, you decide to use Strands, LangChain, LangGraph, or another orchestration framework, it simply becomes the layer above your Atlas API—not a replacement for it.

Then responds:

Tesco Milton Keynes unloading procedure:
Use Gate 3 goods entrance.
