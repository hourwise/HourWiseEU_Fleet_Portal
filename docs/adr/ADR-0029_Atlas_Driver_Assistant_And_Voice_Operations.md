# ADR-0029: Atlas Driver Assistant And Voice Operations

## Status

Proposed

## Context

HourWise already treats Atlas as more than a chatbot. Atlas is intended to become the operational interface that explains fleet data, highlights risk, and coordinates safe actions across the Fleet Portal and Driver App.

The Driver App can benefit from Atlas because drivers often need fast access to approved company knowledge, customer instructions, site procedures, job context, route warnings, and compliance advice while away from the office.

However, the driver environment is safety-critical. Atlas must not become a distracting free-form AI interface, especially while a vehicle is moving.

## Decision

HourWise will add Atlas to the Driver App as a controlled driver assistant backed by the Atlas Operations API, company knowledge base, job context, and permission checks.

The Driver App will not send free-form requests directly to an LLM or to the Fleet Portal. Requests will go through an approved backend API:

```text
Driver App
  -> Atlas Request API
  -> permissions, audit, context resolution
  -> company/site/job knowledge lookup
  -> safe response generation
  -> Driver App text and optional spoken reply
```

Supabase Realtime and push notifications may deliver responses and follow-up events, but they are delivery channels, not the Atlas brain.

## MVP Capabilities

Capability IDs:

- `ATLAS-DRIVER-001`: Driver Knowledge Assistant
- `ATLAS-DRIVER-002`: Site Procedure Lookup
- `ATLAS-DRIVER-003`: Driving-Safe Voice Mode
- `ATLAS-DRIVER-004`: Job Context Awareness
- `ATLAS-DRIVER-005`: Approved Action Suggestions
- `ATLAS-DRIVER-006`: Customer Arrival Notification Workflow
- `ATLAS-DRIVER-007`: Driver Context Engine
- `ATLAS-DRIVER-008`: Audit And Permission Boundary

The first version should support:

- typed driver questions
- push-to-talk voice input
- short spoken responses
- approved company handbook/procedure lookup
- approved customer/site procedure lookup
- current job and location context where available
- simple read-only answers
- no always-listening wake word
- no free-form customer messaging
- no autonomous action without confirmation

## Driver Context Engine

Atlas should use context so the driver does not need to explain everything manually.

The context engine may include:

- current driver
- current company
- current shift
- assigned vehicle
- assigned trailer
- current job
- current customer
- current site
- route plan
- current location, if available
- planned ETA
- remaining driving time
- remaining working time
- current compliance warnings

Example:

```text
Driver: Atlas, what do I need to know when I arrive?
```

Atlas should infer the destination from the current job/route context and return the approved site procedure.

## Driving-Safe Mode

If the vehicle is moving or the driver is in an active driving context, Atlas must use driving-safe behaviour:

- spoken answers preferred
- short answers only
- no long document reading
- no typing required
- large repeat/stop/call office controls
- no complex choices
- no distracting UI
- full procedure can be queued for reading when stopped

Example:

```text
Tesco Milton Keynes: use Gate 3. Report to the transport office. Hi-vis required. Wait for banksman before reversing. I will show the full procedure when stopped.
```

## Approved Action Suggestions

Atlas may suggest limited approved actions, but actions must be confirmed by the driver or manager.

Example:

```text
Atlas: DPD Hub 2 requires arrival notice 10 minutes before gate entry. You are approximately 12 minutes away. Would you like me to send the approved arrival message?
Driver: Yes.
System: Approved template sent and logged.
```

The system must use templates for customer-facing messages at first. Free-form AI-generated customer messages are out of scope for MVP.

## Security And Permissions

Atlas Driver Assistant must enforce:

- company isolation
- driver assignment checks
- job/site visibility checks
- document approval/current-version checks
- audit logging for requests and responses
- no service-role access from the Driver App
- no direct SQL generated by an LLM
- no unrestricted customer communication

Atlas should only answer from approved sources when giving procedural or compliance-sensitive information.

## Future Vision

Future phases may include:

- proactive site arrival prompts
- customer ETA workflows
- spoken delay reporting
- voice acknowledgement of route updates
- incident/defect voice capture
- multilingual spoken answers
- Teams/WhatsApp/voice-channel Atlas interfaces
- offline cached approved procedures
- customer-specific operational workflows

## Consequences

Positive:

- The Driver App becomes more useful without adding office complexity.
- Drivers can access current approved procedures quickly.
- Atlas becomes a practical operational assistant, not just a management chatbot.
- Site/customer instructions can be delivered safely in the cab.

Negative:

- Voice and driving-safe UX require careful design.
- The knowledge base must have approval/versioning.
- Incorrect or stale procedure answers could create safety risk.
- Permission and audit boundaries must be implemented before broad rollout.

## Rejected Alternatives

### Direct Supabase push to Atlas as the main architecture

Rejected. Realtime/push is suitable for delivery, but Atlas requests need permission checks, context resolution, audit logging, and controlled tool access.

### Free-form LLM chat in the Driver App

Rejected for MVP. Driver operations are safety-critical and should start with approved knowledge lookup and controlled actions.

### Always-listening voice assistant

Rejected for MVP due to distraction, privacy, cost, and false activation risk.

## Final Decision

HourWise will add Atlas to the Driver App as a controlled, audited, driving-safe operational assistant. Atlas will use the Atlas Operations API, approved knowledge sources, and driver/job context. Realtime/push may deliver responses, but all intelligence and action execution must pass through backend permission and audit controls.
